# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are Persian-first luxury home and decor buyers, interior designers, and customers looking for distinctive decorative pieces for elevated homes, villas, studios, and hospitality spaces. They shop for sculptures, wall paintings, luxury dinnerware, candlesticks, wall and table clocks, side tables, dining tables, luxury vases, console mirrors, TV tables, and similar statement pieces.

Users arrive with different levels of confidence and budget. Some are collectors or design professionals expecting a high-level brand experience; others are everyday buyers who want access to luxury without feeling excluded or intimidated.

## Product Purpose

Moein Antik is a luxury antique and decor e-commerce platform that presents curated home pieces with enough trust, detail, and polish to support online purchase decisions. The product exists to make the catalog feel premium, credible, and easy to browse while supporting the full shopping journey: discovery, product detail, wishlist, cart, checkout, payment, order history, and admin operations.

Success means customers feel they are buying from a serious high-level brand, while still feeling welcome and capable of purchasing from the website.

## Brand Personality

Luxury, trustworthy, elevated.

The brand should feel like another level of purchase: refined, confident, curated, and worth trusting with expensive decorative items. It should raise the perceived value of Moein Antik without making the experience cold, distant, or only for elite customers.

## Anti-references

Do not make the storefront feel cheap, crowded, generic, marketplace-like, or template-made. Avoid visual patterns that make the brand look like an ordinary discount shop, a mass product catalog, or a decorative theme pasted onto a basic store.

Do not make luxury feel inaccessible. The design should avoid intimidating exclusivity, overly dark showroom drama, unreadable delicate typography, or anything that makes average buyers feel the shop is not for them.

## Design Principles

- Make every product feel considered: photography, spacing, hierarchy, and descriptions should support the sense of curation.
- Balance aspiration with welcome: the brand should feel premium, but purchase paths must remain clear and approachable.
- Earn trust before asking for payment: surface product detail, availability, pricing, policies, and account states with calm precision.
- Keep Persian-first commerce effortless: RTL layout, labels, errors, forms, and checkout flow should feel native and direct.
- Separate showroom and operations: public pages can feel gallery-like, while admin screens should stay practical, dense, and fast to scan.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive controls. Preserve reduced-motion support for any animation, avoid color-only status communication, keep touch targets comfortable on mobile, and prioritize readable Persian typography for a broad range of shoppers.
